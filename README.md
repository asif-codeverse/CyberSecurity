# **Q1. Examine the term “Social Engineering”. How Does Social Engineering Work?**

**(10 Marks)**

## **Introduction**

Social engineering is one of the most dangerous cyber attack techniques because it targets **human psychology rather than technical systems**. Attackers manipulate people into revealing confidential information or performing unsafe actions.

---

## **Definition of Social Engineering**

**Social engineering** is a technique used by cyber criminals to trick individuals into disclosing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by exploiting trust, fear, curiosity, or urgency.

---

## **How Social Engineering Works**

Social engineering attacks usually follow a systematic process:

### **1. Information Gathering**

The attacker collects information about the victim from social media, websites, or public records.
_Example:_ Email ID, job role, contacts.

---

### **2. Building Trust**

The attacker pretends to be a trusted person or authority.
_Example:_ Bank official, IT support staff, government employee.

---

### **3. Creating Urgency or Fear**

Victims are pressured to act quickly.
_Example:_ “Your account will be blocked today.”

---

### **4. Exploitation**

The victim is tricked into sharing sensitive data or clicking malicious links.

---

### **5. Execution of Attack**

The attacker uses the obtained information for fraud, identity theft, or system access.

---

## **Common Types of Social Engineering**

- Phishing
- Vishing (voice phishing)
- Smishing (SMS phishing)
- Pretexting
- Baiting

---

## **Conclusion**

Social engineering exploits human behavior rather than system vulnerabilities. Awareness, verification, and cautious behavior are the best defenses against such attacks.

**Examples:**

- Online gambling

**Examples:**

- Cyber terrorism
- Attacks on government websites
- Espionage and data leakage

Cybercrime affects individuals, organizations, society, and governments in different ways. Classification of cybercrime helps in understanding threats and framing appropriate laws and security measures.

# **Q6. What is a Botnet? How Does a Botnet Work? Explain the Types of Botnets and Types of Botnet Attacks**

**(10 Marks)**

## **Introduction**

Botnets are one of the most powerful tools used by cybercriminals to carry out large-scale cyber attacks without the victim’s knowledge.

---

## **What is a Botnet?**

A **botnet** is a network of compromised computers or devices (called bots or zombies) that are remotely controlled by an attacker known as a **botmaster**.

---

## **How a Botnet Works**

1. Malware infects victim devices.
2. Infected devices connect to the attacker’s command server.
3. The attacker sends commands remotely.
4. Bots perform malicious activities simultaneously.

---

## **Types of Botnets**

### **1. Centralized Botnet**

Bots communicate with a single command and control (C&C) server.

---

### **2. Decentralized (Peer-to-Peer) Botnet**

Bots communicate with each other without a central server, making detection difficult.

---

### **3. IoT Botnet**

Uses infected IoT devices like smart cameras and routers.

---

## **Types of Botnet Attacks**

### **1. Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)**

Overwhelms a server with traffic to make it unavailable.

---

### **2. Spam Distribution**

Botnets send large volumes of spam emails.

---

### **3. Data Theft**

Stealing sensitive information from infected systems.

---

### **4. Click Fraud**

Generating fake clicks on advertisements for financial gain.

---

## **Conclusion**

Botnets pose a serious threat to cyber security by enabling large-scale attacks. Understanding how botnets work helps in designing effective detection and prevention mechanisms.

Cyber criminals use proxy servers and anonymizers to hide their identity and avoid being traced.

# **Q1. Discuss about DoS and DDoS Attacks**

**(12 Marks)**

## **Introduction**

Denial of Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks are common cyber attacks aimed at making a system, network, or service unavailable to legitimate users by overwhelming it with excessive traffic or requests.

---

## **What is a DoS Attack?**

A **Denial of Service (DoS)** attack is an attack in which a **single system** sends a large number of requests to a target server, exhausting its resources and preventing legitimate access.

### **Characteristics of DoS**

* Originates from one source
* Targets bandwidth, memory, or CPU
* Easy to detect but harmful

---

## **What is a DDoS Attack?**

A **Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)** attack is a more advanced form of DoS where **multiple compromised systems (botnets)** attack a single target simultaneously.

### **Characteristics of DDoS**

* Uses thousands of infected devices
* Very difficult to trace
* Causes large-scale service disruption

---

## **Types of DoS / DDoS Attacks**

1. **Volume-Based Attacks**
   Flood the network with massive traffic
   *Example:* UDP Flood

2. **Protocol Attacks**
   Exploit protocol weaknesses
   *Example:* SYN Flood

3. **Application Layer Attacks**
   Target specific applications
   *Example:* HTTP Flood

---

## **Impact of DoS and DDoS Attacks**

* Website downtime
* Financial loss
* Loss of customer trust
* Disruption of critical services

---

## **Prevention Measures**

* Firewalls and IDS/IPS
* Traffic filtering
* Load balancing
* DDoS mitigation services

---

## **Conclusion**

DoS and DDoS attacks pose serious threats to online services. While DoS attacks originate from a single source, DDoS attacks are more dangerous due to their distributed nature. Strong network security and monitoring are essential to defend against such attacks.
