Helps individuals make decisions based on honesty, fairness and compassion instead of greed or fear.

### 3. Harmony in Relationships

### 4. Sustainable Development

Encourages responsible use of natural resources and protection of environment.

### 5. Ethical Professional Life

Prevents unethical practices by developing ethical competence and social responsibility.

---

In today’s changing scenario, value education is necessary to ensure harmony within self, family, society and nature. It leads to continuous happiness and sustainable prosperity.

# **Q1. What are the Four Orders? Explain their Anusangita and Natural Characteristics.**

**(12 Marks)**

## **Introduction**

Nature is organized in a systematic way. It consists of **four orders**, which are interconnected and mutually fulfilling. Each order has its own natural characteristics and role in the larger system.

---

## **The Four Orders of Nature**

### **1. Physical Order (Padartha Avastha)**

Examples: Soil, water, air, minerals, mountains, rivers.

### **Natural Characteristics:**

- Composition and decomposition
- Stability
- Governed by physical laws
- No consciousness

This order provides the base for all other orders.

---

### **2. Bio Order (Pranic Avastha)**

Examples: Plants and vegetation.

### **Natural Characteristics:**

- Growth
- Self-organization
- Reproduction (seed-based continuity)
- Nurturing role

Plants convert physical matter into usable food and oxygen.

---

### **3. Animal Order (Jeev Avastha)**

Examples: All animals and birds.

### **Natural Characteristics:**

- Will to live
- Instinct-based behavior
- Reproduction (breed-based continuity)
- Dependence on bio order

Animals maintain ecological balance.

---

### **4. Human Order (Gyan Avastha)**

Examples: Human beings.

### **Natural Characteristics:**

- Self-awareness
- Ability to understand and decide
- Desire for happiness and prosperity
- Need for right understanding

Humans can choose to live in harmony or disharmony.

---

## **Anusangita (Interconnectedness / Mutual Fulfillment)**

Anusangita means **mutual interdependence and fulfillment among the four orders**.

- Physical order supports bio order (soil, water).
- Bio order supports animal and human orders (food, oxygen).
- Animal order helps maintain ecological balance.
- Human order must protect and enrich all other orders.

All orders are interconnected and none exists in isolation.

---

## **Conclusion**

The four orders of nature function in harmony through mutual fulfillment. Disharmony arises only when humans fail to understand their responsibility.
